# Amazon Usability Bug Hunt

## Tester

Amanda Bramble

## Date

March 11, 2026

## Objective

Explore Amazon's website to identify usability issues, confusing interactions, or potential bugs.

---

# Finding 1 – Search Results Overload

**Type:** Usability Issue
**Severity:** Low

**Description:**
Search results pages contain a very large number of promoted products and sponsored listings before showing organic results.

**Impact:**
Users may have difficulty quickly identifying relevant products.

---

# Finding 2 – Filter Options Can Be Overwhelming

**Type:** Usability Issue
**Severity:** Low

**Description:**
When searching for products, the number of available filters can become overwhelming for new users.

**Impact:**
Users may struggle to narrow down results efficiently.

---

# Finding 3 – Inconsistent Product Image Sizes

**Type:** UI Issue
**Severity:** Low

**Description:**
Some product listings display images at different sizes, which causes inconsistent alignment within search results.

**Impact:**
This can make the interface appear cluttered.

---

# Finding 4 – Limited Feedback When Filters Apply

**Type:** UX Issue
**Severity:** Low

**Description:**
When filters are applied, there is minimal visual feedback indicating which filters are currently active.

**Impact:**
Users may not realize which filters are affecting their results.

---

# Summary

This exploratory bug hunt focused on identifying usability and UI issues within Amazon's search functionality. Additional testing could include checkout flow, product comparison, and mobile usability.
